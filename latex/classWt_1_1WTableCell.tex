\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
A container widget that represents a cell in a table. 

A \mbox{\hyperlink{classWt_1_1WTable}{W\+Table}} provides a table of W\+Table\+Cell container widgets. A W\+Table\+Cell may overspan more than one grid location in the table, by specifying a \mbox{\hyperlink{classWt_1_1WTableCell_ae14b338d8395de33d93f7a28ef2b3edd}{row\+Span }} and \mbox{\hyperlink{classWt_1_1WTableCell_a0f4912b4c023b78e6de41561e2e0a62a}{column\+Span }}. \mbox{\hyperlink{structTable}{Table}} cells at overspanned positions are hidden. You cannot directly create a W\+Table\+Cell, instead, they are created automatically by a table.

A W\+Table\+Cell acts as any other \mbox{\hyperlink{classWt_1_1WContainerWidget}{W\+Container\+Widget}}, except that both the vertical and horizontal alignment of contents may be specified by \mbox{\hyperlink{classWt_1_1WContainerWidget_aba13c9e62c74bcba53519ee9cf41f40a}{set\+Content\+Alignment()}}.

\doxyparagraph*{C\+SS}

The widget corresponds to the H\+T\+ML {\ttfamily $<$td$>$} or {\ttfamily $<$th$>$} tag, depending on whether the cell is a plain cell or a header cell. The widget does not provide styling, and can be styled using inline or external C\+SS as appropriate.
